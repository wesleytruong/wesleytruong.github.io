Fun Facts About Coffee

Although many people make fun of coffee nerds, it is actually a really fun hobby with that is both interesting and rewarding. There are also tons of different methods of coffee-making, but my personal favorite method is making pourover coffee since it is cheap(er) than other methods and can make a really clear and delicious cup. If you are also interested in the world of coffee, here are some fun facts for you to get to know about it.

Question Answer
What is the difference between Arabica and Robusta coffee? Arabica beans tend to have a sweeter, softer taste, with tones of sugar, fruit, and berries. Robusta, however, has a stronger, harsher taste, with a grain-like overtone and peanutty aftertaste.
How does caffeine affect the body? Caffeine is a stimulant that acts on the central nervous system, increasing alertness and reducing fatigue.
What is the best way to store coffee beans? Store them in an airtight container at room temperature, away from heat, light, and moisture.
What is the ideal water temperature for brewing coffee? The ideal water temperature for brewing coffee is between 195째F and 205째F (90째C and 96째C).
What is the difference between a latte and a cappuccino? A cappuccino has a more even distribution of espresso, steamed milk, and foamed milk, while a latte has much more steamed milk and a light layer of foam.
How is decaf coffee made? The most common methods involve using solvents, water, or carbon dioxide to extract caffeine from the beans.
What is "single-origin" coffee? Single-origin coffee is coffee grown within a single known geographic origin, such as a single farm or a specific collection of beans from a single country.
What is the history of coffee? Coffee was first discovered in Ethiopia in the 11th century. It then spread to the Middle East and eventually to Europe and the Americas.
What are the health benefits of drinking coffee? Coffee is a good source of antioxidants and has been linked to a reduced risk of several diseases, including Parkinson's, type 2 diabetes, and liver disease.
What is the environmental impact of coffee production? Coffee production can have a significant environmental impact, including deforestation and water pollution. However, sustainable and shade-grown coffee practices can help to mitigate these effects.
